This chapter presents the outcomes of the various evaluations conducted throughout the development of the web-based University of San Carlos Patient Information System (USC-PIS). It covers the results of the functional testing (Unit and Integration), non-functional testing (Performance and Security), and User Acceptance Testing (UAT). The findings validate the system’s reliability, efficiency, and usability in modernizing the clinic's healthcare delivery.

\section{Functional Testing}

Functional testing was conducted to verify that both isolated modules and integrated workflows behaved exactly as specified in the software requirements. The testing pipeline was divided into Unit Testing and Integration Testing, ensuring rigorous validation of clinical logic and role-based constraints.

\subsection{Unit Testing Results}
Unit testing evaluated the foundational components and security middleware of the USC-PIS. A total of six primary unit tests (UT-01 to UT-06) were executed to validate clinical logic and database security.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Database Security (UT-01):} Validated the PostgreSQL \texttt{pgcrypto} column-level encryption. The test successfully confirmed that sensitive patient data (Names and Diagnoses) bypassed plain-text memory and were stored strictly as \texttt{BinaryField} (\texttt{bytea}) hashes.
    \item \textbf{Clinical Logic \& Filtering (UT-02 \& UT-04):} Verified the advanced Academic Year-Level filtering logic, achieving 100\% accuracy in isolating 1st to 4th-year students. Additionally, the dental module successfully enforced regex validation for the FDI tooth notation, strictly accepting the 11-48 numerical range and blocking invalid inputs like ``1,2''.
    \item \textbf{System Integrity (UT-03, UT-05, UT-06):} Validated the Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) expiration logic, the SafeList fast-pass onboarding which correctly auto-assigned DOCTOR and NURSE roles, and the File Security audit which maintained a 100\% rejection rate against malicious payloads such as \texttt{.EXE} and \texttt{.JS} scripts. All unit tests passed without failure.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Integration Testing Results}
Integration testing (IT-01 to IT-05) verified that different modules, such as frontend forms, backend APIs, and external enterprise integrations, worked seamlessly together.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Clinical Pipeline (IT-01):} Simulated the end-to-end medical certificate workflow (USC Form ACA-HSD-04F). The test successfully verified the sequence from a Nurse's draft to a Doctor's approval, culminating in the automated generation of the landscape-formatted PDF.
    \item \textbf{Role-Based Access Control (IT-03):} Executed a security stress test on the RBAC middleware. The system accurately restricted cross-patient access, returning 403 (Denied) or 404 (Hidden) HTTP statuses for unauthorized users attempting to access role-gated endpoints.
    \item \textbf{Automated Workflows (IT-02 \& IT-05):} Verified the real-time feedback analytics dashboard and the health campaign dissemination. The system successfully displayed administrative health alerts directly to student feeds and persisted post-visit survey data into the database.
\end{itemize}

\section{Non-Functional Testing}

To ensure the system could handle peak clinical loads and address the operational inefficiencies of the legacy paper-based method, rigorous non-functional performance testing (PT-01 to PT-04) was conducted.

\subsection{Performance Benchmarking Results}
The USC-PIS was benchmarked against strict latency and concurrency thresholds:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Search Efficiency (PT-04):} To resolve the inefficiency problem highlighted in Chapter 1, the patient search latency was tested on a seeded database of over 100 patient records. The search query returned results in \textbf{127.81 ms}, operating nearly four times faster than the 500 ms maximum allowable limit.
    \item \textbf{PDF Generation (PT-01):} The dual-engine PDF module (utilizing \texttt{xhtml2pdf} with a \texttt{reportlab} fallback) successfully generated official Medical Certificates in \textbf{122.93 ms}, functioning well below the 1000 ms limit constraint.
    \item \textbf{Concurrency \& Overhead (PT-02 \& PT-03):} The system's architecture stabilized while processing 20 simultaneous HTTP requests in \textbf{0.66 seconds}, suffering a 0\% data drop rate. Furthermore, the \texttt{pgcrypto} encryption integration proved highly efficient, registering a computational overhead of \textbf{< 1.0 ms} during database commits.
\end{itemize}

\section{User Acceptance Testing (UAT) Results and Feedback}

User Acceptance Testing (UAT) was conducted to gather qualitative and quantitative feedback, assessing whether the platform met the intended objectives of both the clinic staff and the student body.

\subsection{Clinic Staff Evaluation}
Testing was conducted with a clinical sample of N=7 staff members, consisting of two Doctors, three Nurses, and two Dentists. The majority of the respondents (5 out of 7) reported using the system on a daily basis. Based on the statistical means measured on a 5.00-point Likert scale, the staff provided moderate to positive feedback, reflecting both operational improvements and the transitional challenges of adopting a new digital workflow.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{System Efficiency \& Accuracy:} The system achieved a mean score of 3.29/5.00 for efficiency in managing patient records, while data accuracy and the prevention of duplicate records both scored higher at a mean of 3.57/5.00. Staff respondents noted that the platform provides an ``Easy and fast system flow'' and makes ``generating data'' significantly easier. Doctors explicitly praised the organization of patient profiles, stating that the interface makes ``everything a lot more organize[d]''.
    \item \textbf{Reporting \& Workflow Integration:} The generation of reports and the provision of tools for analyzing patient data both received a mean score of 3.29/5.00. During testing, Nurses stated that while the reporting system is efficient, ``adding a feature to sort students by academic year would further improve our ability to generate monitoring reports''. This direct qualitative feedback led to the engineering of the ``Application Retooling'' dynamic filter bar. However, the system's integration with existing manual workflows scored a mean of 3.14/5.00, and the ability to reduce the need for external tools scored 2.71/5.00, indicating that staff still heavily rely on parallel physical documentation.
    \item \textbf{Overall Satisfaction \& Constructive Feedback:} The general satisfaction among clinic staff reached a mean of 3.29/5.00. While many users praised the streamlined data gathering, one Doctor provided highly critical feedback, citing concerns over ``No security features to protect confidential data'' and the continued necessity of ``written records for documentation and possibly, medico-legal purposes''. This critical feedback is particularly valuable; while it reflects a user misunderstanding of the system's invisible backend security, it strongly validates the architectural necessity of the PostgreSQL \texttt{pgcrypto} column-level encryption implemented by the researchers. Furthermore, it highlights a critical need to better educate medical staff on digital security protocols during the training phase.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Student Evaluation}
UAT was conducted with N=70 students from the CPE 1202L course to evaluate the platform’s accessibility, responsiveness, and feature relevance. Based on the survey responses and statistical means (measured on a 5.00-point Likert scale), the system received overwhelmingly positive feedback.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Accessibility \& Usability:} Students consistently highlighted the system's clean user interface and accessibility. The system scored an exceptional mean of 4.70/5.00 for the ease of logging in and accessing personal records. The clarity of the health information displayed was rated even higher, achieving a mean of 4.76/5.00. Furthermore, system responsiveness and the ability to access the platform anytime and anywhere both achieved a high mean score of 4.61/5.00, indicating a highly stable and available web architecture.
    \item \textbf{Feature Relevance \& Satisfaction:} The student body overwhelmingly identified the platform's core functionalities as highly beneficial, awarding a mean score of 4.60/5.00 for overall feature helpfulness. Specifically, the automated Medical Certificate request and issuance process received a mean of 4.47/5.00, while the timeliness of system notifications scored 4.49/5.00. In qualitative feedback, users specifically praised the Patient Medical Dashboard and the automated BMI Calculation tools, noting that the ability to order a medical certificate without queueing in physical lines was a massive operational improvement.
    \item \textbf{Overall Satisfaction \& Constructive Feedback:} The general satisfaction with the USC-PIS reached an impressive mean of 4.76/5.00. While the system was highly praised, the most recurring constructive suggestions from the demographic were requests for a ``Dark Mode'' toggle and further optimizations for mobile responsiveness to prevent interface stretching. Additionally, several users noted that the strict password creation requirements during registration were slightly confusing and could benefit from clearer prompt instructions. These suggestions have been documented to guide future post-deployment maintenance.
\end{itemize}
