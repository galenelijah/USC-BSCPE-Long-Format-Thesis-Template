\chapter{Comprehensive Training Plan (Deliverable D4): USC-PIS}
\label{appendix:training_plan}

\section{Program Overview}

\textbf{Objectives:} The primary objective is to equip all users with the digital competency required to operate the fully digital USC-PIS, reducing patient record retrieval time to well under 1 second (capitalizing on the 127.81ms search benchmark).

\textbf{Delivery Methods:} Training will be executed via a hybrid delivery approach, using hands-on demonstration modules supplemented by the comprehensive User Manuals.

\section{System Adoption Strategy (Addressing Bottlenecks)}

Initial database audits revealed critical system underutilization (e.g., 3 medical records for 9 patients, 1 dental record, and 0 file uploads). To solve this adoption issue, training will incorporate:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Workflow Integration:} Practical demonstrations on incorporating the software directly into the clinic's daily operational workflows.
    \item \textbf{Incentives \& Benefits:} Showcasing how digital record-keeping eliminates redundant paperwork and provides instant clinical insights.
\end{itemize}

\section{Module 1: Clinical Operations (For Doctors \& Nurses)}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Patient Intake:} Staff will learn to utilize the \textbf{Application Retooling Filter Bar} to segment patient datasets in real-time by \textbf{Role, Program, Year Level, and Registration Period}. Instruction will explicitly cover the Academic Year and Semester logic (e.g., filtering 1st Semester from August 1 to December 31) to route targeted student groups.
    \item \textbf{Record Management \& Security:} Users will master the \textbf{Unified Record Component} for entering vitals and the mandatory "Concern / Reason for Visit" fields. A highly technical segment will focus on the \textbf{\texttt{pgcrypto} encryption architecture}, explaining how patient diagnoses and names are automatically hashed as secure \textbf{\texttt{BinaryField}} strings via backend signals.
\end{itemize}

\section{Module 2: Specialized Clinical Workflows (For Dentists)}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Dental Charting \& Streamlined Entry:} Dentists will be instructed on charting tooth conditions using the \textbf{FDI notation (11-48)} numerical range, strictly enforced via regex. To accelerate high-volume clinic days, Dentists will master the \textbf{"Consultation Only" mode}, which makes full diagnoses optional, and utilize the explicit "Referral To" field.
\end{itemize}

\section{Module 3: Administrative Output (For Staff \& Admins)}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Certificate Issuance:} Staff will learn the workflow for generating the \textbf{ACA-HSD-04F Medical Certificate} directly from a health record. Training will emphasize that while Nurses may draft the certificate, the system mandates secure approval executed solely by the \textbf{Doctor} before the PDF is rendered.
    \item \textbf{Reporting:} Staff will practice generating \textbf{Visit Trends} and \textbf{Treatment Outcome Reports} using the Celery-powered asynchronous reporting module, achieving data outputs in under 1 second without blocking the interface.
\end{itemize}

\section{Module 4: Student Engagement (For Students \& Faculty)}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Personal Health Access \& Feedback:} Students will be trained on navigating their personalized \textbf{Student Medical Record (SMR)} via the \textbf{/health-insights} tab to view non-interactive timelines. They will also learn how to respond to automated post-visit emails by submitting evaluations and interacting with Health Campaigns.
\end{itemize}

\section{Assessment \& Evaluation}

\textbf{Training Success \& Adoption Metrics:} Rather than utilizing formal proficiency exams, the success of the training program and system adoption will be evaluated during the Pilot Deployment phase (targeting 70\% of 1st-year CpE students and selected clinic staff). The evaluation will be based on two realistic pillars:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Direct User Feedback \& Issue Reporting:} Success will be measured by qualitative feedback gathered from the clinic staff and pilot students regarding the system's intuitiveness, as well as tracking any reported bugs or workflow bottlenecks.
    \item \textbf{Real-World System Performance:} Evaluating whether trained staff can execute operational tasks efficiently under real clinic loads, ensuring that benchmarks, such as the sub-500ms patient search latency and rapid PDF generation, are maintained during daily operations.
\end{itemize}

\chapter{USC-PIS User Manuals}
\label{appendix:user_manuals}

\section{Manual 1: Clinical \& Administrative Staff Guide}

\textbf{Target Audience:} Doctors, Nurses, Dentists, and Clinic Staff.

\subsection{1. Secure Onboarding}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Navigate to the registration portal and enter your pre-authorized clinic email address.
    \item The system automatically cross-references the email against the backend \textbf{SafeEmail} table.
    \item Upon validation, the system bypasses the 6-digit Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) step and instantly assigns your professional clinical role.
\end{enumerate}
\textbf{Technical Note: RBAC \& Security:} Staff authorizations are globally protected via Django REST Framework \textbf{\texttt{PermissionClasses}}. Access to patient databases is strictly gated to medical personnel, neutralizing unauthorized data exposure.

\subsection{2. Advanced Patient Discovery}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Navigate to the \textbf{Patients} module and use the global search bar to query a patient's Name, Email, or USC ID.
    \item Click the \textbf{Filters} button to expand the Application Retooling Filter Bar.
    \item Apply the \textbf{Academic Year} filter (e.g., 2025-2026), \textbf{Semester} logic (1st Sem: Aug-Dec, 2nd Sem: Jan-May), and \textbf{Year Level} to isolate specific student groups.
\end{enumerate}
\textbf{Technical Note: Search Efficiency:} Database query optimizations verify that patient search results among 100+ records are returned with a latency of \textbf{127.81ms}, nearly four times faster than the benchmark limit.

\subsection{3. The Unified Record Workflow}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Medical Documentation:} Navigate to the \textbf{Medical Records} tab, input the mandatory "Concern / Reason for Visit", record vital signs, and click "Save".
    
    \textbf{Technical Note:} Upon saving, PostgreSQL's \textbf{\texttt{pgp\_sym\_encrypt}} function hashes sensitive patient data into a secure binary format invisibly in the background (< 1.0ms overhead).
    
    \item \textbf{Dental Documentation:} Navigate to the \textbf{Dental Records} tab and use the \textbf{FDI 11-48} numerical system to input tooth conditions. For rapid checks, use the streamlined "Consultation/Referral" mode.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{4. Medical Certificate Management \& Issuance}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Navigate to \textbf{Medical Certificates -> Create New Certificate}.
    \item Select the patient; the system will autonomously autofill their Name, Year Level, and Full Course Name.
    \item Select the \textbf{USC Clinic Template ACA-HSD-04F} and enter the "Purpose/Requirement".
    \item A \textbf{DOCTOR} must assess the draft, select Medical Fitness Status ("Fit"/"Not Fit"), and authorize approval. The dual-engine PDF system will instantly render the certificate for download.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{5. System Maintenance \& Data Backups}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Generating a Backup:} To ensure the safety of clinical records, Administrative Staff can trigger a full system backup via the Database Monitor page.
    \item \textbf{Mechanism:} The system captures all active records (Medical history, dental charts, and user profiles) and packages them into a portable JSON format.
    \item \textbf{Storage:} It is recommended to download and store this compressed file in a secure university-approved location monthly to ensure a permanent offline copy of the clinic's digital database.
\end{itemize}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Health Insights (\texttt{/health-insights}):} Review unified medical and dental timelines. Document attachments listed within the unified timeline are rendered non-interactive to enforce strict data privacy.
    \item \textbf{Enterprise Reporting (\texttt{/reports}):} Access available analytical templates (e.g., Visit Trends, Feedback Analysis).

PDF generation utilizes a Dual-Engine strategy (\textbf{\texttt{xhtml2pdf}} with a \textbf{\texttt{ReportLab}} fallback) and exports are programmatically capped at the first 200 items per section to maintain document stability. Excel/CSV exports offer unlimited deep data analysis.

\section{Manual 2: Student \& Faculty Portal Guide}

\textbf{Target Audience:} Patients (Students and Faculty).

\subsection{1. Identity \& MFA}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Navigate to the registration portal and enter an official university email address.
    \item The system enforces strict domain validation; the email must end in \textbf{\texttt{@usc.edu.ph}}.
    \item Check the university inbox for the automated \textbf{6-digit verification code} dispatched via the Gmail API.
    \item Input the code into the portal before the 24-hour expiration threshold to activate your account.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{2. The Student Medical Record (SMR)}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Log into the portal and navigate to the \textbf{Patient Medical History} link in the sidebar (\textbf{\texttt{/health-insights}}).
    \item The interface displays a Unified Timeline separated into Medical Records, Dental Records, and Health Insights.
    \item Click the \textbf{Health Insights} tab to view personalized health analytics, monthly visit frequency patterns, and visual trend indicators.
\end{enumerate}
\textbf{Technical Note: Data Privacy:} The Student Medical Record dashboard is governed by strict access controls limiting visibility exclusively to the record owner and authorized medical staff. All clinical documents are proxied securely through an authenticated backend stream.

\subsection{3. Health Awareness}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Navigate to the \textbf{Campaigns} tab (\textbf{\texttt{/campaigns}}) in the navigation sidebar.
    \item Browse active health awareness campaigns, which are categorically organized.
    \item Select a campaign to open the \textbf{Universal Document Viewer}.
    \item Interact with high-quality image PubMats via zooming, or execute direct, secure downloads of attached health guidelines.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{4. The Feedback Loop}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Exactly 24 hours following the conclusion of a Medical or Dental consultation, the system automatically dispatches a Feedback Request reminder to your university email.
    \item Open the email and click the embedded rating links, or log directly into the portal and navigate to the \textbf{Feedback} tab.
    \item Select the specific clinical visit to evaluate.
    \item Input a 1-5 star "Overall Experience" rating, answer Yes/No to the "Staff Courtesy" metric, and provide qualitative comments.
\end{enumerate}
\textbf{Technical Note: Feedback Constraints:} To ensure data integrity, the system architecture implements a \textbf{\texttt{UniqueConstraint}} on the backend. This restricts users to exactly one feedback submission per clinical visit, preventing skewed satisfaction analytics.

\section{Software Quality Assurance (SQA) Audit Summary}
The system’s operational efficiency was rigorously evaluated under the quantitative parameters defined in the performance test suite (PT-01 to PT-04). The benchmarks confirm that the architectural optimizations successfully eliminated legacy latency issues. Specifically, search query optimizations yield a latency ranging strictly between \textbf{123.16ms and 127.81ms}, nearly four times faster than the 500ms threshold. Furthermore, the dual-engine PDF module generates official Medical Certificates in \textbf{122.93ms to 124.53ms}, safely below the 1000ms limit.

To guarantee reliability during peak registration, concurrency was audited via test PT-02. The architecture successfully handled \textbf{20 simultaneous HTTP requests in 0.66 seconds} with a 0\% data drop rate. Additionally, a cybersecurity audit (UT-06) confirmed a \textbf{100\% rejection rate} against high-risk extensions (.EXE, .JS, and .PY), neutralizing potential system vulnerabilities.

\section{Preliminary User Acceptance Testing (UAT) Summary}

\subsection{Staff Evaluation}
Preliminary User Acceptance Testing (UAT) was conducted utilizing feedback from a clinical sample of Clinic Staff (N=7, comprised of Nurses, Doctors, and Dentists). The quantitative and qualitative returns indicate strong efficiency improvements compared to the legacy manual operations. Staff explicitly noted that "generating data is easier" and that the workflow is "fast and easy". Early testing bottlenecks, such as the request to add a feature to sort students by academic year, were immediately verified and resolved during the Application Retooling phase. One Doctor highlighted rigorous expectations for data privacy and the necessity of documentation security, fully validating the architectural decision to integrate PostgreSQL \texttt{pgcrypto} for encrypted clinical records.

\subsection{Student Evaluation (UAT Results)}
Preliminary User Acceptance Testing (UAT) was conducted with the student demographic to evaluate the system's usability, accessibility, and feature relevance. Based on the survey responses, the system received overwhelmingly positive feedback, with the vast majority of students rating their overall satisfaction at 4 or 5 out of 5.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Usability \& Accessibility:} Students consistently highlighted the system's clean user interface and smooth navigation, giving exceptionally high ratings for the ease of logging in and the ability to access their records anytime and anywhere.
    \item \textbf{Highest Rated Features:} When asked which features were most useful, the student body overwhelmingly praised the \textbf{Patient Medical Dashboard}, the \textbf{Medical Certificate Issuance}, and the automated \textbf{BMI Calculation} tools. Students noted that "ordering" a medical certificate without waiting in physical queues was a massive operational improvement.
    \item \textbf{Constructive Feedback \& Roadmap:} While technical issues were minimal, the most recurring constructive feedback was a request for a \textbf{"Dark Mode"} toggle and better responsiveness to ensure the application fits perfectly on narrower mobile screens.
\end{itemize}
