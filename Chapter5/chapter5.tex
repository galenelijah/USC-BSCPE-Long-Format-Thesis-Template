\section{Summary}

The development of the web-based University of San Carlos Patient Information System (USC-PIS) was undertaken to modernize the USC clinic's legacy paper-based operations. The project aimed to establish a centralized, secure, and highly efficient digital platform for managing clinical records, issuing medical certificates, disseminating health campaigns, and automating patient feedback.

The system was engineered utilizing a modern three-tier architecture: a React 18 frontend, a Django 5.0.2 backend, and a PostgreSQL database utilizing vendor-aware logic for dual-environment deployment. To ensure comprehensive system reliability, the project underwent a rigorous Software Quality Assurance (SQA) audit encompassing functional testing, non-functional performance benchmarking, and security validation. Following system stabilization, a pilot deployment was structured to target 70\% of the students currently enrolled in CPE 1202L (Data Structures and Algorithms), along with selected clinical staff, to validate real-world operational readiness.

\section{Conclusions}
The implementation of the web-based University of San Carlos Patient Information System (USC-PIS) successfully modernized the legacy paper-based operations of the USC clinic. The system transitioned fragmented daily treatment records into a centralized, secure, and highly efficient digital ecosystem.

The empirical data collected during performance benchmarking definitively proves that the architectural optimizations resolved prior administrative inefficiencies. The system's advanced search query logic processes patient records with an average latency of 127.81ms—operating nearly four times faster than the maximum acceptable threshold of 500ms. Furthermore, the custom dual-engine PDF module renders official Medical Certificates (USC Form ACA-HSD-04F) in 122.93ms, ensuring rapid administrative output. The high-availability architecture also proved highly resilient, successfully managing 20 concurrent requests in just 0.66 seconds with a 0\% failure rate.

From a usability standpoint, the system was overwhelmingly embraced by its end-users. The student demographic reported an exceptional overall satisfaction mean of 4.76 out of 5.00. Clinic staff confirmed that the transition to digital charting significantly improved the accuracy and reliability of stored clinical data, yielding a 3.29 out of 5.00 overall satisfaction. Ultimately, the USC-PIS is a fully matured, production-ready system that successfully digitalizes clinical workflows while maintaining strict data privacy, proving to be a massive operational improvement for the university clinic.

\section{Recommendations}
Based on the testing results, design constraints of the current deployment, and the constructive feedback gathered during the User Acceptance Testing (UAT), the following recommendations are proposed for future iterations of the USC-PIS:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{User Interface Enhancements:} While the system was highly praised for its clean navigation, multiple student respondents requested the addition of a ``Dark Mode'' toggle and suggested further UI optimizations to ensure the interface is perfectly responsive on narrower mobile screens.
    \item \textbf{Expansion of Clinical Scope (Inventory Management):} As identified in the original project scope, complex inventory management and patient medication tracking were deferred due to time constraints. Future developers should integrate a robust medication inventory module to track clinic stock, monitor supply alerts, and automatically link dispensed medications directly to the patient's unified timeline.
    \item \textbf{Automated Appointment Scheduling:} The current system strictly handles consultations via a manual walk-in workflow. Future iterations should implement an online appointment scheduling and queue management module, allowing students to book specific time slots with available clinic doctors, thereby reducing physical waiting times in the clinic lobby.
    \item \textbf{Billing and Financial Management Integration:} To transition the platform into a comprehensive enterprise Health Information System (HIS), future deployments should integrate a billing module capable of handling comprehensive patient billing, payment tracking, and insurance processing for faculty and staff.
    \item \textbf{Enterprise System Integration:} The current system utilizes a custom, highly secure authentication flow to accommodate standard cloud hosting constraints. Future iterations should aim to integrate the official University Single Sign-On (SSO) infrastructure and advanced Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) to further streamline the login process for students and faculty.
    \item \textbf{University-Wide Deployment:} Following the successful pilot deployment targeting the students enrolled in CPE 1202L, it is recommended that the system be gradually rolled out to all remaining departments and colleges within the university to maximize the impact of the health campaigns and centralized record-keeping.
    \item \textbf{Provision of Dedicated Clinic Hardware and Security Training:} As evidenced by the critical qualitative UAT feedback regarding medico-legal documentation, medical professionals have highly valid security concerns and are rightfully reluctant to access sensitive Electronic Medical Records (EMRs) using their personal mobile devices. To address these hardware realities and achieve full digital adoption, it is highly recommended that the university provides dedicated, secure workstations or tablet devices exclusively for clinic staff use. Furthermore, training programs must include comprehensive modules that transparently explain the system’s backend \texttt{pgcrypto} encryption measures to build digital trust among medical professionals.
    \item \textbf{Mobile Application and Telemedicine Capabilities:} As the system scales, transitioning the web platform into a Progressive Web App (PWA) with offline capabilities, push notifications, and telehealth features (such as remote video consultations) will significantly expand the university's healthcare delivery footprint.
    \item \textbf{Expansion of Dental Charting Logic:} Version 1.0 of the system’s regex backend currently restricts charting strictly to permanent adult teeth (FDI notation 11-48) to prevent data entry errors. Future iterations must expand this logic to accept the 51-85 ranges to fully support pediatric and deciduous dental charting, ensuring complete alignment with the official USC Dental Health Record requirements.
\end{enumerate}
