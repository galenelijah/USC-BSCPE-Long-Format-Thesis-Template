\begin{abstract}
The modernization of the University of San Carlos (USC) Clinic’s Patient Information System (PIS) aims to resolve significant operational inefficiencies caused by legacy paper-based workflows. The previous system struggled with fragmented health records, slow retrieval times, and limited capabilities for health information dissemination and data-driven reporting. This project deployed a robust, three-tier web-based architecture utilizing React 18, Django 5.0.2, and PostgreSQL, specifically designed to digitalize daily treatment records, consultation management, and medical certificate issuance (USC Form ACA-HSD-04F). To ensure strict compliance with data privacy regulations, the system integrates the \texttt{pgcrypto} extension, enforcing column-level symmetric encryption (AES-256) for all Protected Health Information (PHI).

Rigorous Software Quality Assurance (SQA) audits and performance benchmarking demonstrated exceptional system reliability. The optimized database architecture successfully handled search queries with an average latency of 127.81ms, operating nearly four times faster than the 500ms maximum acceptable threshold. Furthermore, the custom PDF rendering module generated official medical certificates, optimally rendering between 122.93ms and 124.53ms. During User Acceptance Testing (UAT), the system garnered an overwhelmingly positive response, with the student demographic (N=70) reporting an overall satisfaction mean of 4.76 out of 5.00. Ultimately, the USC-PIS is a fully matured, production-ready system that successfully digitalizes clinical workflows, enhances administrative efficiency, and significantly improves the quality of healthcare delivery for the university community.
\end{abstract}
