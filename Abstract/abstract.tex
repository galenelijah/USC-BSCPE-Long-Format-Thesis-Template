\begin{abstract}
Previous clinic operations at the University of San Carlos (USC) Clinic relied heavily on a fragmented, paper-based system, leading to inefficiencies in data retrieval, limited reporting capabilities, and ineffective health information dissemination. To address these challenges, this study developed the USC-DC Patient Information System (USC-PIS), a comprehensive, web-based platform designed to centralize and modernize healthcare delivery for the university. The system was engineered using a three-tier architecture utilizing React 18 for the responsive presentation layer, Django 5.0.2 for the application layer to enforce strict Role-Based Access Control (RBAC), and PostgreSQL for the relational data layer. To ensure maximum data privacy, the architecture integrated PostgreSQL's pgcrypto extension, applying military-grade, column-level symmetric encryption to safeguard Protected Health Information (PHI).

The platform’s operational reliability was validated through a rigorous Software Quality Assurance (SQA) audit, achieving a 100% pass rate across all functional unit and integration tests. Non-functional performance benchmarking demonstrated exceptional efficiency that resolved manual bottlenecks, registering patient search query latencies between 123.16ms and 127.81ms, and generating official Medical Certificates via a dual-engine PDF module in 122.93ms to 124.53ms. User Acceptance Testing (UAT), conducted alongside a structured pilot deployment targeting 80% of 1st-year students from the Bachelor of Science in Tourism Management (BSTM) and selected clinic staff, yielded highly positive evaluations. Clinic staff highlighted massive improvements in monitoring efficiency via the dynamic "Application Retooling" filter, while students praised the accessibility of the Patient Medical Dashboard and the convenience of requesting medical certificates without enduring physical queues. Ultimately, the USC-PIS proved to be a highly secure, production-ready system that successfully digitalized clinical workflows and significantly enhanced the operational efficiency of the university clinic.
\end{abstract}
